The opening lines, “it busies itself with hoarding the heat and the sunlight” makes me think of a young eager farmhand, busy preparing himself for the Summer season. Kotzin’s exchange between the sunlight and the goldenrod makes you wonder what plants would think and say if they had the capacity to do so. I enjoy her interpretation of this relationship.
