I enjoyed reading into the Amish lifestyle and watching Lucy become the woman whom she wrote about in her poem, “Hannah Binding Shoes.” At first, I questioned the image of the vase and what it possibly stood for. But the ending works to drive Lucy’s plight home. I found it to be the most moving passage. The vase itself is a cool, stark image. The flowers in the vase are bright, but their life is short. Their joy is fleeting. Lucy’s redeeming moment occurs when she realizes that the flowers, which parallel the quick success of the youthful Miss Hamilton, are nothing when compared to the everlasting object that holds them.
