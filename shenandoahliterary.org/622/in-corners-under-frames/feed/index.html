I really enjoyed it, mostly because I thought it was a kind of adventure, moving to a new place, but I also remembered thinking that if I ever grew up and got really rich, I’d buy every single house I’d ever lived in. (Obviously not feasible, but it made sense to 12-year-old me!) For some reason, we grow incredibly attached to the dwellings we spend our years in, a message this poem conveys as well. The woman’s desire to visit her old home and reminisce is one common to most people, I think. I particularly like the moment where the author’s mother-in-law notices a brass hook were her husband used to hang up his coat. It reminds us that often, no matter how much things change, there will always be remnants of what came before.
