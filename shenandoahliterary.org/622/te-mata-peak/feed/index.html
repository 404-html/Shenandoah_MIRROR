Comments on: Te Mata Peak https://shenandoahliterary.org/622/te-mata-peak/ Wed, 04 Jul 2018 20:18:16 +0000 hourly 1 https://wordpress.org/?v=5.1.1 By: Sam O'Dell https://shenandoahliterary.org/622/te-mata-peak/#comment-188 Mon, 18 Mar 2013 23:51:06 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/622/?p=106#comment-188 This poem’s constant juxtaposition of nature with distinctly man-made elements really made it stand out for me. Every time the author takes a moment to describe the beauty of the place she is in, she stops to interject some modern-day technology into the moment. As they walk up the mountain, someone takes a picture of it. At the top, they read a man-made plaque. As “an exaltation of larks soars above,” she hears music from nearby. This constant weaving of imagery from the natural world along with the man-made helps to emphasize the fragility of nature and man’s role in protecting it.
