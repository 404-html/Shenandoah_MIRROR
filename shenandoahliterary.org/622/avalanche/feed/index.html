It is particularly effective in causing an “avalanche” effect inside the poem: the rhythm rolls steadily faster because of the way each stanza is connected. The way the last line is a throw back to the first stanza causes this rhythm to grind to an abrupt halt, just as a rock falling down a cliff eventually comes to rest at the bottom.
