However, I think it is adds a unique and effective layer of complexity to the poem. The syntax and structure of the lines also relates to the nature of “gossip.” The lines appear to run on and sound conversational. This concept of loyalty also seems fragile, personal, and sacred. Just as the “glass” imagery suggests, it is easily shattered. The final lines, “The dream that you are walking over glass/to reach the other man/You don’t tell that to anyone,” reinforce that fragility.

It even sounds like a chain: some lines are enjambed while others are end-stopped, giving this poem a chatty tone that correlates to the theme of women gossiping, and to the image of hens pecking. I also appreciate how it articulates the implied social structure that goes along with this gossip, but the poem also has a surprising twist at the end with the implication that actual disloyalty or genuinely gossip-worthy material should be kept silent.
