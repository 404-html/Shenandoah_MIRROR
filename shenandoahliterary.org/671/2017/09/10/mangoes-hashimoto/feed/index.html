There is nothing particularly laboring about any of the words alone, but together the poem exudes an itchy harshness through specific details such as the “Chevrolet’s tires [churning] with dust” and “the three black heads / of [her] family” as they disappear without looking back. The “shattered fruit” and the familiar and unfavorable feeling of “fruit flies [hovering] low” followed by the punchy declaration: “I don’t like Hawaii” similarly convey disturbance and the tense confusion of a child left behind. The use of the present tense effectively brings us into this scene of childhood, abandonment and stinging cuts in our mouths.
