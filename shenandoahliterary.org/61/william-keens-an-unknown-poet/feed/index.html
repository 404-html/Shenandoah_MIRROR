We had a long discussion one night at the Pickwick about what the moral thing to do with our lives would be. ]]> By: Jon Salm https://shenandoahliterary.org/61/william-keens-an-unknown-poet/#comment-132 Mon, 14 Nov 2011 17:03:01 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/61/?p=29#comment-132 For me, the most interesting aspect of this piece is not in its content, but in its presentation. It is very innovative how Adcock combines a personal nonfiction essay with an analysis of Keens’ poetry. While the essay starts out as a straight analysis, it markedly changes when Adcock asks, “Why write about a book that will never appear in a bookstore? And who is William Keens?” This transition helps contextualize the poetry and explain exactly why the poems are notable. While the poems are great on their own, the more traditional essay-like section towards the end is the strongest part of this piece.
