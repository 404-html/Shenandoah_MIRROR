]]> By: nashe16 https://shenandoahliterary.org/641/2014/08/15/debris/#comment-585 Mon, 15 Dec 2014 15:43:21 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/641/?p=48#comment-585 There is something hilarious and almost charming about the idea of a rifle-wielding old woman who takes the law into her own hands. There is also something disturbing about the idea of a woman who insists on giving burials to the animals that drown in her pool but is also willing to shoot a man simply because she suspects him of being criminal. This is one of my favorite elements of Noir: nothing (aside from the film) is black and white. The reader isn’t sure whether Campbell is actually a criminal, and therefore whether Emily is justified in her actions. What’s more, we even begin to question our own moral compasses as we attempt to justify the murder of a young man. Noir thrives in uncertainty, and “Debris” left me unsure.
