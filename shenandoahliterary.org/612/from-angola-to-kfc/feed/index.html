Comments on: From Angola to KFC https://shenandoahliterary.org/612/from-angola-to-kfc/ Just another Shenandoah site Sat, 30 Jan 2016 19:53:33 +0000 hourly 1 https://wordpress.org/?v=5.1.1 By: Tim McAleenan https://shenandoahliterary.org/612/from-angola-to-kfc/#comment-139 Wed, 29 Feb 2012 18:59:10 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/612/?p=85#comment-139 I really like this piece of work from Jackson Lassiter, particularly the last part titled “Original Or Extra-Crispy?” I really like the anaphora with the word “recognize” that discusses how Sholanda does not recognize the relationship between her ancestors and fried chicken, hip hop, and her roots. Lassiter does a fantastic job of highlighting the importance of “agency” to add a layer of irony to the story. Sholanda’s roots would give her a type of claim on the atmospherics of her environment–the chicken, hip hop, etc.– yet, they serve as tools to oppress her rather than liberate her because she does not command any ownership over these ancestral accoutrements. This is a great commentary on personal identity and how one’s own cultural reportoire can be turned against oneself.
