]]> By: Christina Proctor https://shenandoahliterary.org/621/ode-to-girl-with-hand-on-barbed-wire/#comment-66 Wed, 03 Oct 2012 20:57:57 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/621/?p=285#comment-66 After I finished reading the first paragraph of this story, I knew that I was going to love the writer’s style. I could immediately picture myself in my own car, arm stuck out the window, feeling both simultaneously propelled and thwarted by the oncoming wind. Widner’s narrative does an excellent job of drawing the reader in immediately; we want to root for Letty and the little girl. The subtle descriptions of the girl’s foster home conditions juxtaposed with Letty’s conflicting and somewhat ambiguous feelings about her own job combine to give the brief story a heavy, yet meaningful tone. One of the strongest elements of Widner’s piece is its ability to provide a fleeting look into the “vague relationship” between Letty and the girl, and also provoke the reader to think about what happens on either side of the narrative. How did Letty end up in this career path? What happens to the little girl and her grubby hand gripping the business card?
