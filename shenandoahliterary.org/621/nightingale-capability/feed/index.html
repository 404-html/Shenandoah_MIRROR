Of course–it’s more complicated than that–but this poem elbows Keats in the ribs sweetly–by suggesting there are fleeting moments we may steal away from Truth in the service of love and happiness. The real beauty of the this negatively capable poem is that both Keats and the writer embrace paradox–but while Keats tips his drink to the dark side of it–Wrigley tilts his wine to the light.
