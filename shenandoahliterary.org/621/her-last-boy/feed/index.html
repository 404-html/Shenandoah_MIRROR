]]> By: Collier https://shenandoahliterary.org/621/her-last-boy/#comment-122 Tue, 09 Oct 2012 14:23:36 +0000 http://shenandoahliterary.org/621/?p=287#comment-122 Her Last Boy, by Thomas McConnell, moved me deeply. The beginning emits the sweet southern relationship between a grandmother and her grandson. But as the story continues, her monologue becomes bittersweet. The reader can hear the pain in her voice and see the horrors of her past. The grandmother represents a character that all readers wish a happy ending upon. But the story cultures a fear in readers, as they fear the possibility of living through experiences similar to hers. Because of this, I almost wanted to distance myself from her to lesson the pain. Her pain is one every parent fears. No one hopes to bury a child they bore into the world, much less watch him experience pain they can’t alleviate.
